RESOANTE

About the project

Resonate is an app that helps people explore and share new music playlists from around the world associated with different moods (emotions and feelings) and activities. Users can "heart" a playlist to boost it's ranking on the charts.


 The story behind the idea: We came up with the idea of Resonate while having a conversation about Bollywood movies. One of my friends, in a sarcastic tone, asked me "Why do all Bollywood movies have songs in them?”

I explained him how songs in Bollywood movies act as MacGuffin and help with the storytelling aspect of the movie. Songs in Bollywood movies help the audience to build an emphatical connection with the plot of the movie. For example, a romantic music video is played during the beginning of a new love story in order to make the audience build that passionate emotion in their minds.

This led us to the conversation about current music. We discovered a problem. Modern music is getting emotionally ambiguous day by day and there are not a lot of ways to discover music that you could associate emotions with.

Problem: There is no real way to discover new music based on moods, activity or even location.

The solution: We created Resonate which helps users discover newly recommended songs based on moods, activity, and location preferences. 

Timeline: 8 weeks

The team: Meet Palan, Rob Nashed, Clay Tercek, Woo Song

My role: Product Design, Visual Design, User Experience Design and Research, and ReactJS development

Identifying who we are designing for

Whether young or old, everyone listens to music but we wanted to focus on people who use online music streaming services Spotify, Apple Music, Pandora, etc. Research showed that people between the age of 14 to 39 (mostly students and young professionals) listen to music online so we set our targeted audience to be students and young professionals.

BUT…HOW DO WE VISUALIZE THESE MOODS AND EMOTIONS?

Studying the best ways to visually describe moods

After a lot of research and talking to designers, we found that the best way to visualize mood and emotions in music is by using color and motion

Thinking of a concept to bring moods, colors and motion all together

We decided to go with the four most used social mood tags: happy, sad, calm and energetic and placed them corresponding to the color people associate those moods with. To further filter/customize your playlists search/creation, you can add more theme and activity tags and also a location. The playlist would then appear with a background of the color you selected so whrn users look at the colors while lsitening to music in the playlist, the colors reflects the moods they selected.

FINAL DESIGNS

Map: Search or pin your preferred location on the map and see the top charts in that place.

Playback: Heart your favorite playlists to boost their rankings and get them on the top charts. Star specific songs to save them in your library.

Browse: Find songs, albums, playlists, artists and much more. Not sure? No problem! Explore new music based on locations or use the mood picker to discover new music based on your mood, feelings or activity.

Create a new playlist: Find your favorite songs from the search and just drag the album cover down to add the song to your playlist and drag the cover up to remove a song. You can also give your playlist a location, a color based on the mood or an activity tag. Making new playlists is now fun and intuitive.

Color/Mood picker: Color/mood picker was a concept we came up with to visualize moods with the help of color and motion. You can select your mood and the playlist will automatically get a background color corresponding to your mood on the mood/color picker.

Settings: Your resonating profile has options to import/export music from/to your spotify account.
